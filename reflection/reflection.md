# Application: [MindDoc]
- Most important difference between pipelines: [The manual pipeline produced more focused personas tied directly to specific review patterns, while the automated pipeline generated broader, more generic personas that occasionally included unsupported assumptions, however was far quicker. The hybrid approach combined the best of both, the quick ideation of automated AI assisted outputs with the accuracy and precision of manual refinement.]
- Most useful pipeline: [The hybrid pipeline produced the most balanced outputs because it preserved the automation speed while correcting vague requirements and improving persona grounding.]
- Most surprising finding: [Several automated requirements used measurable-sounding language but lacked concrete thresholds, making them difficult to validate in practice. This lead to completely changing a number of them]
- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline: [Many automated test cases were broad, high-level goals for the system like "The system shall have an intuitive and user-friendly interface that allows users to easily navigate and access features." rather than specific, measureable, non-ambiguous requirements compliant to IEEE 29148.]
